Assorted Sushi Platter

A high-end platter featuring fresh salmon and tuna sashimi, classic California rolls, and savory nigiri.

Prep: 60 min | Cook: 0 min (Rice: 30 min)
Servings: 4-6
Difficulty: Hard

Sushi Rice & Seasoning

  • Sushi Rice (short-grain) 2 cups
  • Rice Vinegar 1/4 cup
  • Granulated Sugar 2 tbsp
  • Salt 1 tsp

Platter Fillings

  • Sushi-Grade Salmon (sliced thin) 8 oz
  • Sushi-Grade Tuna (sliced thin) 8 oz
  • Nori Sheets (seaweed) 6-8 sheets
  • Avocado (sliced) 1 large
  • Cucumber (julienned) 1/2
  • Wasabi, Pickled Ginger, Soy Sauce For serving

Instructions

  1. Cook and Season the Rice: Rinse the sushi rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Cook according to your rice cooker or stovetop instructions. While hot, gently fold in a mixture of the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Allow the rice to cool to body temperature—**do not refrigerate**.

  2. Prepare Fish and Vegetables: Ensure your fish is sliced against the grain and kept very cold. Slice the avocado and cucumber into long, thin strips suitable for rolling. Set up a station with a damp cloth (to clean your hands) and a small bowl of water (for dipping fingers).

  3. Make Maki (Rolls): Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo mat. Lightly wet your hands, take a handful of rice, and spread it evenly over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top. Arrange a thin strip of fish, cucumber, and avocado across the center. Roll the mat tightly away from you, pressing gently. Wet the empty nori edge to seal the roll.

  4. Make Nigiri: Cut thin, 2-inch slices of fish (salmon and tuna). Dip your finger in water, grab a small amount of rice (about 1.5 tablespoons), and form it into an oval, finger-sized cushion. Place a slice of fish on top, and use a small dab of wasabi between the fish and rice to hold it together.

  5. Assemble Platter: Use a sharp, damp knife to slice the maki rolls into 6-8 pieces. Arrange the rolls and nigiri attractively on a platter. Serve immediately with pickled ginger, wasabi, and high-quality soy sauce.

Nutritional Facts (per serving, approx.)

Calories 420 kcal
Protein 30 g
Fat 15 g
Carbs 40 g

Approximate values based on a mixed serving of rolls and nigiri.

Chef's Notes & Tips

The quality of your ingredients is everything for sushi. Always use **sushi-grade** fish from a reputable source. Keep a bowl of water with a touch of vinegar nearby to dip your knife before slicing the rolls—this prevents the rice from sticking and tearing the nori.
