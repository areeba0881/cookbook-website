Ultimate Gourmet Burger

A juicy, perfectly seasoned beef patty topped with caramelized onions, sharp cheddar, and a secret aioli sauce.

Prep: 20 min | Cook: 15 min
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef (80/20 Chuck) 1.5 lbs
  • Brioche Buns 4
  • Yellow Onion (sliced) 1 large
  • Sharp Cheddar Cheese (slices) 4
  • Mayonnaise 1/4 cup
  • Dijon Mustard 1 tbsp
  • Garlic (minced) 1 clove
  • Fresh Lettuce and Tomato As needed

Instructions

  1. Make Aioli: In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.

  2. Caramelize Onions: Slice the onion thinly. Melt butter in a skillet over low heat and add the onions. Cook slowly for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply brown and soft.

  3. Form Patties: Gently divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 6 oz each). Form into patties slightly wider than the buns, pressing a small indentation in the center of each to prevent bulging.

  4. Cook Burgers: Season patties generously with salt and pepper. Grill or pan-fry over medium-high heat for 3-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or longer as desired. Place a slice of cheddar on each patty during the last minute of cooking, covering with a lid to help it melt.

  5. Toast Buns and Assemble: Lightly butter the brioche buns and toast them until golden brown. Spread the garlic aioli on both halves of the bun. Place the burger patty on the bottom bun, top with caramelized onions, lettuce, and tomato, then place the top bun on top. Serve immediately.

Nutritional Facts (per serving)

Calories 680 kcal
Protein 45 g
Fat 40 g
Carbs 35 g

Approximate values based on ingredients listed.

Chef's Notes & Tips

Do not overwork the meat when forming the patties, as this leads to a tough, dense burger. For the best flavor, mix a pinch of smoked paprika or Worcestershire sauce into the ground beef before forming the patties.
