Decadent Chocolate Cake

A rich, moist, and intensely chocolate cake, perfect for birthdays and special occasions.

Prep: 30 min | Cook: 35 min
Servings: 12
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients (Cake)

  • All-purpose Flour 1 3/4 cups
  • Granulated Sugar 2 cups
  • Unsweetened Cocoa Powder 3/4 cup
  • Baking Soda 1 1/2 tsp
  • Salt 1 tsp
  • Eggs (large) 2
  • Buttermilk (or milk with vinegar) 1 cup
  • Vegetable Oil 1/2 cup
  • Hot Coffee (or hot water) 1 cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans. Line the bottoms with parchment paper.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt. Make sure there are no lumps.

  3. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, buttermilk, and oil until combined.

  4. Combine Batters: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix on medium speed for about 2 minutes. Carefully stir in the hot coffee (the batter will be thin).

  5. Bake: Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a wooden toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely before frosting.

Nutritional Facts (per slice)

Calories 450 kcal
Protein 6 g
Fat 25 g
Carbs 55 g

Approximate values, including basic chocolate frosting.

Chef's Notes & Tips

Using **hot coffee** instead of water enhances the flavor of the cocoa powder dramatically, resulting in a deeper, richer chocolate taste without making the cake taste like coffee. For the most decadent result, use a high-quality Dutch-process cocoa.
